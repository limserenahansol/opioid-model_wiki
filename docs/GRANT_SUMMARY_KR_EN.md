## EN

We hypothesize that mice do not solve progressive-ratio schedules by explicit representation of the full requirement structure. Instead, opioid-seeking persistence is governed by a **low-dimensional latent motivational process** integrating recent reward, effort, failure, and pause. **Contingent** opioid experience (active group) reshapes update rules of reward/value (`V_t`) and deficit/withdrawal (`D_t`) states, producing stronger persistence and **V×D amplification** at re-exposure. **Non-contingent** exposure (passive group) builds Pavlovian context memory (`C_t`) and can elevate withdrawal seeking via **generalized gain (PIT-like)**, modeling a patient-relevant passive-to-instrumental vulnerability route. We will fit staged models M0–M3 to lick-level data using simulation-based inference.
